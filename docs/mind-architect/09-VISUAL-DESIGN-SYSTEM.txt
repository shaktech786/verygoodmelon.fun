================================================================================
                      DESIGN MANIFESTO
================================================================================

This game is about THINKING. The UI should feel like:
- A well-worn philosophy professor's desk
- Marginalia in an ancient book
- Index cards spread across a cork board
- Ink bleeding into quality paper
- The warmth of a library at midnight

NOT:
- Glossy mobile game garbage
- Neon-lit sci-fi interfaces
- Generic fantasy card game
- Anything that looks "AI-generated"
- Sterile, corporate, lifeless

GUIDING PRINCIPLE:
Every element should look like it was made by someone who CARES.
Imperfection is beautiful. Texture is trust. Warmth is wisdom.


================================================================================
                     VISUAL IDENTITY
================================================================================

THE METAPHOR: THE PHILOSOPHER'S STUDY

Imagine a cluttered study in a centuries-old university:
- Dark wood desk with coffee rings
- Stacks of paper with handwritten notes
- Cork boards with pinned index cards
- Leather-bound books with gold lettering
- A single warm lamp casting soft shadows
- Ink wells, quills, and worn erasers

This is our world. Translate it to pixels.

TEXTURE PHILOSOPHY:

Every surface has subtle texture:
- Paper grain (not smooth white)
- Ink that bleeds slightly at edges
- Shadows that feel hand-painted
- Borders that aren't perfectly straight
- Colors that feel mixed by hand

NOTHING IS PERFECTLY ALIGNED:
- Cards sit at slight angles (Â±1-2Â°)
- Text has micro-variations in weight
- Lines have imperceptible wobble
- Spacing breathes, not uniform

FONT SELECTION:

PRIMARY DISPLAY: "Playfair Display"
- Used for: Titles, card names, boss names
- Weight: 700 (bold) or 400 (regular italic)
- Why: Elegant serifs, high contrast, timeless authority
- Fallback: Georgia, Times New Roman

BODY TEXT: "Source Serif 4"
- Used for: Descriptions, dialogue, effects
- Weight: 400 (regular), 600 (semibold for emphasis)
- Why: Highly readable, warm character, scholarly feel
- Fallback: Georgia, serif

UI TEXT: "IBM Plex Sans"
- Used for: Buttons, labels, numbers, stats
- Weight: 400, 500, 600
- Why: Clean but warm, good for data
- Fallback: system-ui, sans-serif

NUMBERS: "IBM Plex Mono"
- Used for: Damage numbers, HP, stats, costs
- Weight: 500, 700
- Why: Tabular numerals align perfectly
- Fallback: Menlo, monospace

- Height: 8px

- Double-line border (like an etching)
- Inner shadow creating depth
- Illustrations are LINE ART style
- Black ink on cream background
- NOT full-color paintings

================================================================================
                    ANIMATION CHOREOGRAPHY
================================================================================

TIMING REFERENCE:
â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€
- Instant: 0-50ms (micro-feedback)
- Quick: 100-200ms (UI responses)
- Standard: 250-400ms (card movements)
- Deliberate: 500-800ms (major transitions)
- Cinematic: 1000ms+ (boss intros, endings)

EASING FUNCTIONS:
- UI elements: ease-out (quick start, soft land)
- Cards moving: cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1) (slight bounce)
- Damage numbers: ease-in (builds momentum)
- Fades: ease-in-out (gentle)

BATTLE TURN FLOW:
â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€

YOUR TURN START:
1. Draw pile rustles (100ms)
2. Cards fly to hand one by one (150ms each, 50ms stagger)
3. Hand settles with micro-bounce
4. "Your Turn" text fades in/out (300ms)
5. TP pips fill with golden glow

PLAYING A CARD:
1. Card lifts from hand (100ms)
2. Card flies to play area (200ms, arc trajectory)
3. Ink particle trail follows card
4. Card lands with micro-bounce (100ms)
5. TP pip dims
6. Damage preview updates

ENDING TURN:
1. Button pulses briefly
2. Cards in play "activate" (glow sequence)
3. Damage calculation builds up
4. Final damage flies to enemy
5. Enemy reacts (hit animation)
6. HP bar depletes (smooth, 300ms)
7. Enemy turn begins

ENEMY TURN:
1. Enemy "charges" (telegraph animation, 500ms)
2. Attack animation (unique per enemy)
3. Impact on player (screen shake)
4. Coherence bar updates
5. Brief pause
6. Your turn begins


================================================================================
                    MICRO-INTERACTIONS
================================================================================

These tiny details create the "premium" feel:

HOVER STATES:
- Cards: Subtle lift + border glow
- Buttons: Lift + shadow deepen
- Enemy: Eye tracking cursor
- Relics: Tooltip with flourish animation

CLICK FEEDBACK:
- Ripple effect from click point
- Button depresses visually
- Subtle haptic (if supported)
- Sound: Soft "click" or "thunk"

LOADING:
- NOT a spinner
- Ink spreading on paper animation
- Text: "Gathering thoughts..."
- Feels thematic, not generic

TRANSITIONS:
- Page changes: Paper turning/sliding
- Modal open: Unfolds like letter
- Modal close: Folds and recedes
- Victory: Triumphant unfurl

IDLE ANIMATIONS:
- Cards breathe slightly (very subtle scale)
- Enemy has idle animation
- Background particles drift (dust motes)
- Candle/lamp flicker (light variation)


================================================================================
                    REDUCED MOTION VERSION
================================================================================

For users with vestibular disorders or motion sensitivity:

DISABLED ANIMATIONS:
- No parallax effects
- No floating/breathing
- No particle systems
- No screen shake
- No swooping transitions

REPLACED WITH:
- Instant position changes
- Simple opacity fades (200ms max)
- Static damage numbers
- Subtle color changes instead of movement

STILL PRESERVED:
- Hover state feedback (non-animated)
- Focus indicators
- Selection states
- Critical information


================================================================================
                    RESPONSIVE BREAKPOINTS
================================================================================

DESKTOP (1200px+):
- Full layout as designed
- 7 cards visible in hand
- Large enemy portrait
- All animations enabled

TABLET (768px - 1199px):
- Condensed spacing
- 5 cards visible, horizontal scroll
- Medium enemy portrait
- Simplified particles

MOBILE PORTRAIT (< 768px):
- Stacked layout
- 3 cards visible, swipe to see more
- Small enemy portrait
- Minimal animations
- Bottom sheet for card details
- Larger touch targets (48px minimum)

MOBILE LANDSCAPE:
- Similar to tablet
- Card hand along bottom
- Enemy and play area share space


================================================================================
                    ACCESSIBILITY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

VISUAL:
- All text meets WCAG 2.1 AA contrast (4.5:1 minimum)
- Focus indicators visible (3px solid, offset)
- No color-only information (patterns, icons too)

- No drag required (click alternative)
- Large click targets
- No time pressure

- Clear, consistent layouts
- Tooltips for all elements
- Undo available
- No sudden changes
- Tutorial replayable


================================================================================
                    ILLUSTRATION STYLE GUIDE
================================================================================

For artists creating game assets:

STYLE: Victorian Scientific Illustration meets Philosophical Marginalia

TECHNIQUE:
- Line art primary (ink on paper)
- Cross-hatching for shading
- NO gradients (except subtle background)
- Visible brush/pen strokes welcome
- Imperfection is character

ENEMIES:
- Abstract representations of concepts
- NOT literal monsters
- Think: How would an 1800s naturalist draw "Confirmation Bias"?
- Use symbolism (tunnels, mirrors, scales)
- Faces can be distorted but not grotesque

CARDS:
- Central illustration relates to concept
- Frame with decorative but not busy border
- Icons are simple, recognizable
- Can include small marginalia (notes, arrows)

AVOID:
- Photorealism
- Generic fantasy art
- Anime/manga style
- Anything that looks "stock"
- Bright, saturated colors
- Busy, cluttered compositions


================================================================================
                    SOUND DESIGN NOTES
================================================================================

AUDIO PHILOSOPHY:
Sound should feel like:
- Library ambiance
- Turning pages
- Ink on paper
- Wooden furniture
- Distant thunderstorm

UI SOUNDS:
- Card draw: Paper sliding
- Card play: Soft thunk on wood
- Button click: Satisfying tactile click
- Hover: Very subtle (almost subliminal)
- Error: Gentle "nope" (not harsh)

COMBAT:
- Damage dealt: Impact + whoosh
- Coherence loss: Glass crack/stress
- Chain bonus: Building chime
- Victory: Relief + triumph swell
- Defeat: Somber fade

MUSIC:
- Ambient, not melodic
- Solo piano or string quartet snippets
- Adapts to battle intensity
- Never overwhelming
- Can be disabled without missing info
