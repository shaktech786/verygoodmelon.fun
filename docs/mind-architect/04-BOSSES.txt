================================================================================
                       BOSS PHILOSOPHY
================================================================================

Bosses are not just bigger biases—they're THE BIG QUESTIONS.
Each boss represents a fundamental philosophical problem that humanity
has wrestled with for millennia.

BOSS DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
1. Test everything learned on that floor
2. Multiple valid strategies (no "correct" answer)
3. Victory feels like genuine philosophical insight
4. Defeat feels like running into a hard truth, not unfairness
5. Memorable encounter that teaches something lasting

BOSS STRUCTURE:
- Multi-phase fights
- Philosophical dialogue between phases
- Puzzle elements mixed with combat
- Alternative victory conditions


================================================================================
                   FLOOR 1 BOSS: THE PROBLEM OF INDUCTION
================================================================================

"Just because the sun rose yesterday, why should it rise tomorrow?"

VISUAL DESIGN:
A massive hourglass with sand flowing upward and downward simultaneously.
The figure inside is David Hume's silhouette, questioning everything.

ENTRENCHMENT: 120 HP (3 phases of 40 HP each)

THEMATIC CORE:
Induction is the basis of all scientific reasoning—we assume the future
will resemble the past. But there's no logical guarantee of this.
This boss tests whether you can justify belief without certainty.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 1: THE PATTERN (40 HP)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"You've observed patterns. You expect them to continue. But why?"

MECHANIC:
Inductive cards (Pattern Matching, Generalization, Analogy) deal DOUBLE damage.
Boss seems weak to your pattern-recognition.

ATTACKS:
┌──────────────────────┬────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NAME                 │ DAMAGE │ EFFECT                                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Past Performance     │   5    │ Standard attack.                           │
│ Regular Intervals    │   0    │ Telegraphs big attack in 3 turns.          │
│ The Expected         │   6    │ Only hurts if you played same card twice.  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

TRANSITION:
When HP hits 0, the boss doesn't die. Instead:

DIALOGUE:
"You relied on patterns. You trusted repetition. But watch..."

The hourglass REVERSES. All your Inductive cards are REMOVED from deck.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 2: THE BREAK (40 HP)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"The sun has risen every day for billions of years. And then, one day..."

MECHANIC:
Inductive cards now deal ZERO damage. The pattern broke.
You must rely on deductive logic or first principles.

ATTACKS:
┌──────────────────────┬────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NAME                 │ DAMAGE │ EFFECT                                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Black Swan           │  12    │ Surprise attack with no warning.           │
│ Broken Regularity    │   7    │ +3 damage per Pattern card in your deck.   │
│ The Unexpected       │   8    │ Occurs when you repeat an action.          │
│ Hume's Fork          │   5    │ Evidence OR Logic, not both, work.         │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

TRANSITION:
When HP hits 0:

DIALOGUE:
"So you abandoned patterns. But can you live without them entirely?"

Your Inductive cards return, but you must choose which to keep.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 3: THE SYNTHESIS (40 HP)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Perhaps the question isn't whether induction is certain,
but whether it is useful enough to act upon."

MECHANIC:
Both Inductive AND Deductive cards deal normal damage.
But you must play them TOGETHER for full effect.
Single-type plays deal 50% damage.

ATTACKS:
┌──────────────────────┬────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NAME                 │ DAMAGE │ EFFECT                                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Pragmatic Truth      │   6    │ Your unused card types deal -3 damage.     │
│ Working Hypothesis   │   8    │ Requires balanced play to mitigate.        │
│ Justified Belief     │   0    │ Heals 10 unless you prove understanding.   │
│ The Useful Illusion  │  10    │ Final attack if fight goes too long.       │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

VICTORY CONDITION:
Defeat by damage OR achieve PRAGMATIC VICTORY:
- Play Induction + Deduction + Evidence in one turn
- Triggers special dialogue and bonus rewards

DEFEAT DIALOGUE:
"You tried to prove too much. Sometimes we must act on less than certainty.
The sun will probably rise. That's enough to plant crops."

VICTORY DIALOGUE:
"You've understood. Induction cannot be proven—only justified by its fruits.
We trust patterns not because we must, but because we cannot live without them.
That trust is not logical certainty. It is something deeper: practical wisdom."

REWARDS:
- Rare card: "Pragmatic Justification" (Use uncertain cards with confidence)
- Relic choice: "Hume's Empiricism" OR "Kant's Categories"
- Insight: +5 max Coherence


================================================================================
                   FLOOR 2 BOSS: THE TROLLEY PROBLEM
================================================================================

"Five will die unless you act. One will die if you do. What is right?"

VISUAL DESIGN:
A literal trolley on tracks, splitting infinitely into variations.
Each track shows different ethical frameworks arguing.

ENTRENCHMENT: 180 HP (3 phases of 60 HP each)

THEMATIC CORE:
The trolley problem tests our moral intuitions about action vs. inaction,
consequences vs. intentions, and the value of human lives.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 1: THE UTILITARIAN (60 HP)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"The math is simple. Five is greater than one. Pull the lever."

MECHANIC:
Boss uses pure numbers. Bigger arguments = more damage.
Rewards quantity over quality.

ATTACKS:
┌──────────────────────┬────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NAME                 │ DAMAGE │ EFFECT                                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Greatest Good        │   8    │ Damage = (5 - cards you played).           │
│ Calculate Lives      │   0    │ Counts your "moral weight" in deck.        │
│ Aggregate Welfare    │   6    │ +2 per card with weight > 3.               │
│ The Numbers Game     │  10    │ Only triggered if you play < 3 cards.      │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

STRATEGY: Play many cards. Quantity matters.

TRANSITION:
"But wait. What if the one person is your child?"

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 2: THE DEONTOLOGIST (60 HP)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"You shall not use a person merely as a means. The one has rights too."

MECHANIC:
Boss punishes using others. Cards that "sacrifice" or "use" deal
Coherence damage to you.

ATTACKS:
┌──────────────────────┬────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NAME                 │ DAMAGE │ EFFECT                                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Categorical Imperative│  7    │ Punishes "using" cards.                    │
│ Duty Calls           │   5    │ Requires you to play specific card type.   │
│ Respect for Persons  │   6    │ Steelman heals enemy (no using others).    │
│ Universal Law        │   9    │ Your selfish plays damage you instead.     │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

STRATEGY: Use principled cards. Quality over quantity. No exploitation.

TRANSITION:
"But if everyone followed that rule, more would suffer..."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 3: THE VIRTUE ETHICIST (60 HP)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"What kind of person do you want to be? What would the virtuous person do?"

MECHANIC:
Boss judges your CHARACTER across the whole fight.
Consistency matters. Changing strategies is punished.

ATTACKS:
┌──────────────────────┬────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NAME                 │ DAMAGE │ EFFECT                                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Character Assessment │  dmg   │ Damage = inconsistencies in your play.     │
│ Practical Wisdom     │   0    │ Rewards if you've been consistent.         │
│ The Flourishing Life │   8    │ Punishes pure calculation.                 │
│ Aristotle's Mean     │   6    │ Extremes (all Logic OR all Evidence) hurt. │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

ALTERNATIVE VICTORY:
- Achieve "Moral Synthesis": Play cards from all 3 ethical traditions
  in one turn (Utilitarian + Deontological + Virtue card)
- This represents finding YOUR ethical framework

VICTORY DIALOGUE:
"There is no universally correct answer to the trolley problem.
What matters is that you've thought deeply about why you choose.
The examined life requires facing these questions—not solving them,
but wrestling with them honestly."

REWARDS:
- Rare card: "Moral Compass" (Choose ethical framework for bonus)
- Relic: "The Golden Mean" (Balance extremes for power)
- School unlock: "Ethicist" path

                   FLOOR 3 BOSS: THE MEANING OF LIFE
================================================================================

"Why does anything matter? Why should you continue?"

VISUAL DESIGN:
A vast void with a single question mark made of stars.
The boss is formless—representing the ultimate question.

ENTRENCHMENT: 250 HP (4 phases)

THEMATIC CORE:
This is the final boss. It represents the deepest question.
Your entire deck, all your choices, everything you've built—
it all comes down to this.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHASE 1: THE NIHILIST (60 HP)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Nothing you do matters. The universe is indifferent. Why struggle?"

MECHANIC:
All damage is reduced by 50%. "What's the point?"
Coherence drains 1 per turn passively.

ATTACKS:

│ The Absurd           │  10    │ Random effect (chaos).                     │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

COUNTER: Keep playing despite futility. Persistence is the answer.

TRANSITION:
"You persist despite meaninglessness. Interesting. But..."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"Pleasure is the only good. Pain the only evil. Why complicate it?"

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"There is no inherent meaning. You must CREATE your own. But can you?"

"Perhaps meaning is neither found nor created. Perhaps it emerges."

FINAL VICTORY CONDITIONS:

OPTION A - PHILOSOPHICAL VICTORY:
- Achieve Philosophy Score of 80+
- This means consistent, thoughtful play throughout the run
- Boss acknowledges your worldview and yields

OPTION B - COMBAT VICTORY:
- Reduce HP to 0 through damage
- Standard but harder path

OPTION C - TRANSCENDENT VICTORY:
- Play "Amor Fati" (Love of Fate) card + Synthesis + First Principle
- Accept everything that happened, synthesize it, ground it in truth
- Instant victory, best rewards, secret ending

FINAL DIALOGUE (varies by victory type):

PHILOSOPHICAL VICTORY:
"You've built something real. Not an answer—a way of living.
The meaning of life is not a fact to discover but a garden to tend.
You have tended yours well. Go now, and live it."

COMBAT VICTORY:
"You've overpowered the question through sheer will to meaning.
Not every philosopher would approve. But you've earned your answer:
To live is to struggle. And struggle itself is enough."

TRANSCENDENT VICTORY:
"Amor fati. You love your fate—all of it, the suffering too.
This is the highest wisdom: not to escape life but to embrace it.
You have become what you were always capable of being.
The game was never about winning. It was about becoming."

REWARDS:
- "Enlightenment" achievement
- Unlock all schools of thought
- Secret card: "Weltanschauung" (Your personal philosophy - custom effect)
- Meta-progression: New Game+ mode


================================================================================
                      BOSS DESIGN PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1. FAIRNESS OVER DIFFICULTY
   - Bosses should be hard but never cheap
   - All attacks are telegraphed
   - Multiple valid strategies exist
   - Failure teaches something

2. PHILOSOPHICAL ACCURACY
   - Boss behaviors reflect real philosophy
   - Counters match actual responses to the problem
   - Dialogue is philosophically meaningful
   - Players learn while fighting

3. EMOTIONAL ARC
   - Phase 1: Confidence (you know what you're doing)
   - Phase 2: Crisis (everything changes)
   - Phase 3: Doubt (is anything true?)
   - Phase 4: Resolution (synthesis or acceptance)

4. MEMORABLE MOMENTS
   - Each boss has a "wow" moment
   - Transitions are dramatic
   - Victory feels earned
   - Even defeat is instructive

5. REPLAY VALUE
   - Different strategies work on different runs
   - Boss dialogue varies based on your deck
   - Alternative victory conditions
   - Secrets to discover
